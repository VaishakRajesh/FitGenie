NUTRITION GUIDE - COMPLETE DIETARY GUIDELINES

1. MACRONUTRIENTS

Proteins (4 calories per gram):
Essential for muscle repair, enzyme production, and immune function.
Sources: Chicken breast, fish, eggs, tofu, lentils, Greek yogurt, whey protein.
Recommendation: 1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight for active individuals.

Carbohydrates (4 calories per gram):
Primary energy source for the body and brain.
Sources: Brown rice, oats, sweet potatoes, quinoa, whole wheat bread, fruits.
Types: Complex carbs (preferred - slow digesting) and Simple carbs (quick energy).
Recommendation: 3-5g per kg of body weight for moderate activity.

Fats (9 calories per gram):
Essential for hormone production, vitamin absorption, and cell health.
Sources: Avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds, fatty fish, coconut oil.
Recommendation: 0.8-1g per kg of body weight.

2. MICRONUTRIENTS

Vitamins and minerals are crucial for health:
- Vitamin D: Sunlight, fatty fish, fortified foods. Important for bone health and immunity.
- Iron: Red meat, spinach, lentils. Essential for oxygen transport.
- Calcium: Dairy, leafy greens, fortified plant milks. Critical for bone density.
- Magnesium: Nuts, seeds, dark chocolate. Helps with muscle function and sleep.
- B12: Animal products only. Vegans must supplement.

3. HYDRATION

- Drink 2-3 liters of water daily (more if exercising or in hot weather).
- Signs of dehydration: Dark urine, fatigue, headache, dizziness.
- Sports drinks are only needed for exercise lasting over 60 minutes.

4. MEAL TIMING

Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before): Light meal with carbs and moderate protein.
Post-Workout (within 2 hours): Protein-rich meal with carbs for recovery.
General: 3-4 balanced meals spaced 3-4 hours apart.

5. WEIGHT MANAGEMENT

Weight Loss:
- Calorie deficit of 300-500 calories per day.
- Prioritize protein to preserve muscle mass.
- Eat more vegetables for volume without many calories.
- Avoid liquid calories (soda, juice, alcohol).

Weight Gain:
- Calorie surplus of 300-500 calories per day.
- Eat nutrient-dense foods, not junk food.
- Multiple smaller meals if appetite is low.

6. SUPPLEMENTATION

Only supplement if diet is insufficient:
- Whey/Casein Protein: Convenient protein source.
- Creatine Monohydrate: 5g daily for strength and power.
- Vitamin D: If limited sun exposure.
- Omega-3: If not eating fatty fish twice weekly.

7. COMMON DIET MYTHS

- "Carbs at night make you fat": False. Total daily calories matter.
- "Detox diets cleanse toxins": False. Your liver does this naturally.
- "Eating fat makes you fat": False. Excess calories cause fat gain.
- "You need to eat every 2-3 hours": False. Meal frequency doesn't affect metabolism.
